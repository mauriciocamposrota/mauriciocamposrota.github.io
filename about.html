About Me

this is my passion.

Mauricio first embraced a love of food as a child, most bountifully at family gatherings while growing up in Lima, Peru. Campos originally planned to be a banker, finishing her bachelor's degree while learning English, but he soon realized his heart was not in it. Campos left the business world behind him and enrolled at Le Cordon Bleue, graduating with a Bachelors degree in culinary arts and more recently received an honorary doctorate degree in culinary arts. With a world of choices ahead, she set out on a tour of countries known for great cuisine. In Paris he took her first job as an apprentice at the Ritz-Carlton, subsequently working his way through Italy, Japan, Korea, Thailand and China. Returning to the U.S., he spent three years under acclaimed chef Pascal Oudin at the Grand Bay Hotel. He continued to hone his chops, and helped prepare a few, doing time at Jeffery Chodorow’s China Grill and Red Square.

In July 2013 Campos took the leap and opened his own restaurant, Food Café, and then Elements Tierra, two successful restaurants in Miami’s Design District that featured an eclectic mix of Latin and Asian-inspired dishes (both sold in 2008). Recognizing a need for great comfort food for stressed-out travelers, in February 2014 he brought his talents as an executive chef to Miami International Airport (in American Airlines terminal) with the opening of Mauricio Campos Cocina, a healthier take on traditional Latin cuisine.

Given his Latin upbringing and charismatic personality, Campos was first offered the chance to showcase his skills on Telemundo, hosting the shows “Vida Gourmet,” “Cocine Mundos,” “De Mañanita” and “El Arte del Buen Gusto,” as well as the Utilisima/FoxLife series “Sazón con Mauricio Campos”, “Lorena en su Salsa” and emmy nominated “El mejor de los peores.” More recently Campos has appeared on NBC’s “Food Fighters” season one and on Food Network’s “Guys Grocery Games.” Campos will be appearing soon on Bravo’s “Recipe for deception” and the 2nd season of NBC’s Food Fighters.

Currently, Campos has accepted the role of consulting producer and acting appearance for Nickelodeon Latin America’s new scripted series “Toni la chef” and the English language series titled “Roni in the kitchen.”
